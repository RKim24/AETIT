The IT teachers at The Academy of Engineering and Technology

Mr. Randall

Mr Randall has a Bachelor's degree in Economics from Western Maryland College and a Master's degree in Computer Science from George Mason University. He currently teaches Junior CS Investigations, AP CS, and Integrated Math II. Prior to becoming a teacher, Mr Randall worked as a software developer for several telecomunications companies.

Mr. Chapin

Education: Graduated from Georgetown University in 1987 with a major in Math and received Masters in Business Administration from the University of Virginia in 1996. Business/Professional: For the last four years I have been teaching Math and Computer Science in Loudoun County Public Schools. I am a career switcher and worked in private industry for 25 years before teaching. I worked in multiple technology roles including serving as a computer systems engineer with Electronic Data systems and co-founding an Internet startup in 1998.

Mr. Poland

Mr. Poland enters his 15th year teaching in LCPS and his first year at the Academies of Loudoun. He spent his first 14 years at Stone Bridge High School teaching Math and Computer Science. He has coached football and basketball at Loudoun County High School and Stone Bridge High School. Mr. Poland received his Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with a Minor in Computer Science at Dickinson College, where he also played football. He earned his Masters Degree in Curriculum and Instruction with a focus of Integrating Technology in the Schools from George Mason University.
